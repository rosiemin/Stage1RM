So why is this page here?

So anyone could tell you the number one way to really procrastinate is to start browsing YouTube and see what deep dark hole of the site you can find. The purpose of this page? Well I'm going to show you some of my favorite past times on YouTube and give you a bit more context into why they are good starting points to lead you to hours of glorious procrastination.

As a cat owner myself, I find all cat videos quite hilarious. Does this make me a slight crazy cat person? I think so, but I really do try to play it off like it's nothing. I mean come on, cats and cucumbers? Who doesn't like something like this? This is just the beginning of any crazy cat videos that will keep you entertained for hours to come.

The BuzzFeed Try Guys are probably some of the most entertaining videos out there, at least in my opinion. I have spent hours upon hours watching the ridiculous s**t that these guys do and really it just leads to me click on more of their videos and before I know it, it's 5:00pm and I should be getting on the subway to go home. Needless to say, these videos always make for a great time for procrastinating.

Last, but certainly not least, I highly recommend any of the late-night shows. Jimmy Fallon, SNL, John Oliver or Stephen Colbert are all good places to start. Everytime I end up loading one of these shows, I spend hours watching lip-sync battles, actual singing battles, random sketches from some pretty hilarious people, or just getting very frustrated about how all the news nowadays is Trump. Either way, it definitely allows for hours of (hopefuly) fun procrasstination.

If all else fails you, then I'd just suggest putting on Netflix, and start to binge watch anything on there. I am only slightly obessed with 30 Rock, but to each their own.
